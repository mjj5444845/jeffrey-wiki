Junjie Ma's research lies in **Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)**, with a methodological focus on the intersection of HCI, Robotics, and Extended Reality. He uses this interdisciplinary toolkit to design and study interactive systems — including Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) environments — as platforms for investigating human behavior and experience.

## Cultural Dimensions of Technology Use

A central theme of Junjie Ma's work concerns how cultural contexts shape collaborative and social settings. Through multimodal expression analysis, cross-lingual cooperative mechanisms, and multilingual interaction design, he examines how cultural differences influence coordination and communication in social media, professional collaboration, and online peer-production communities.

## Multilingual Interaction

Language differences fundamentally alter how people collaborate, share knowledge, and form community. Junjie Ma studies these dynamics across multilingual online and hybrid environments, attending to how speakers navigate between languages in cooperative tasks.

## Cultural AI

A growing dimension of his work concerns **Cultural AI** — the design of culturally sensitive large language models (LLMs) and AI systems capable of understanding and adapting to diverse cultural norms and communicative practices.
